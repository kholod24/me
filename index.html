Why look here?

There are a billion games. A million developers.

I make my games only because I love making games. I pour my pain, anger, envy, and feelings of helplessness into them, just to make it easier.

My games are made so that after playing them, someone might think, "I'm not alone". If that's what you came for, welcome.

Introduction

Here's a little about me. My name is Ilya, and I've been developing games since I was 8 (I'm now 18). I was really inspired by the movie "Indie Game: The Movie" and wanted to make games myself. I first learned to make them in Scratch, then switched to Java. Then the path led me to Unity, and I even managed to participate in a few game jams.

Now I've switched to Godot and am working on a game about "unnoticed efforts" - uncounted.

I'm very inspired by Edmund McMillan and Jonathan Blow. And I hope that I can get at least a little closer to their mastery of game creation.
